Use Cases

Real-world scenarios driving EduCore's design and priorities.

Small District Reporting & Interoperability

Enable small and rural districts to meet state and federal reporting requirements using existing systems. EduCore provides standards-aligned mappings so districts can share and report data without adopting new platforms or infrastructure.

Adult Learner LER Assembly

Help adult learners assemble a comprehensive Learning and Employment Record from fragmented sources — community colleges, workforce programs, employer training, and professional certifications — into a single, portable, standards-based credential profile.

About EduCore

Building the connective tissue for education data standards.

EduCore is a collaborative initiative to create a shared reference architecture for education data interoperability. Rather than building another platform, EduCore focuses on the foundational layer: aligning existing standards, cataloging resources, and providing a common registry that stakeholders across K-12, higher education, workforce development, and credentialing can all build on.

At its core, EduCore is developing a CEDS Ontology with an RDF foundation that maps across the major standards in the ecosystem — including LER, INFUSE, Verifiable Credentials, CASE, Open Badges v3, JEDx, PESC Transcript, and TCP. This shared ontology enables automated federal reporting and powers the next generation of LER-based talent marketplaces.

The project is guided by a Stakeholder Advisory Council and developed in partnership with organizations including 1EdTech and the Ed-Fi Alliance, with explicit alignment to the Learning Interoperability Framework (LIF) and CEDS-EdFi initiatives.
